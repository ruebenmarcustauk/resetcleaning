Airbnb Cleaning Checklist: Turn Your Rental Around in 2 Hours

For Gold Coast Airbnb hosts, the turnover is everything. Cleanliness is consistently the factor guests mention most in reviews, and a single missed detail can cost you a star rating and future bookings. The challenge is doing a hotel-standard turnover in the tight window between checkout and the next check-in. This checklist is built for speed and consistency, so you (or your cleaner) can reset the property in around two hours without missing what guests notice most.

Why short-stay cleaning is different

An Airbnb turnover isn't the same as cleaning your own home. You're working to a deadline, presentation matters as much as hygiene, and you need to restock consumables and stage the space to look photo-perfect. Guests judge cleanliness within seconds of walking in, so the details that signal 'freshly cleaned', crisp linen, a streak-free mirror, a fresh scent, carry enormous weight.

Pro tip

Build a host caddy stocked with everything you need for one turnover: cloths, sprays, fresh linen sets, replacement toiletries, bin liners and a checklist. Grab it and go, and you'll never waste minutes hunting for supplies mid-turnover.

The 2-hour Airbnb turnover checklist

Strip and start the laundry first (minute 0)

The moment you arrive, strip all beds and towels and get a load running (or swap in fresh sets if you have spares). Laundry is your longest task, so starting it first means it runs in the background while you clean.

Kitchen (15-20 minutes)

  • Run or empty the dishwasher; put away all crockery
  • Wipe benchtops, splashback, stovetop and sink
  • Clean the microwave inside and check the oven
  • Empty and wipe the fridge; remove anything guests left behind
  • Restock tea, coffee, sugar and any welcome consumables
  • Empty the bin and replace the liner

Bathrooms (15-20 minutes)

  • Scrub toilet, shower, bath and basin
  • Polish mirror and taps streak-free
  • Replace towels with fresh, neatly folded sets
  • Restock toilet paper, soap, shampoo and amenities
  • Empty bin, replace liner, and check for hair on floors and in the drain

Bedrooms (15 minutes)

  • Make beds with fresh, crisp linen, hotel-style
  • Dust surfaces, bedheads and lamps
  • Check under beds and in drawers for forgotten guest items
  • Open blinds and arrange pillows for a staged look

Living areas (10-15 minutes)

  • Fluff and straighten cushions and throws
  • Dust surfaces and wipe the TV and remotes
  • Reset any welcome book, guides or decor to their proper place
  • Vacuum and mop floors throughout

Final reset and presentation (10 minutes)

  • Empty all bins and take rubbish out
  • Check supplies: toilet paper, paper towel, dishwasher tablets, bin liners
  • Quick scent check; air the place out or use a light, neutral freshener
  • Walk through as if you were the guest arriving, fix anything that catches your eye
  • Photograph the staged space if you spot anything off for your listing

The details that win 5-star cleanliness reviews

Guests notice the small things: hair in the shower or on floors, smudged mirrors, dusty surfaces they run a finger across, and any lingering smell. Nail these and you'll consistently earn top cleanliness scores. A folded toilet-paper point and neatly fanned towels cost nothing but signal genuine care. This is the essence of hotel-standard cleaning.

Frequently asked questions

How do professional Airbnb cleaners handle linen?

Many short-stay cleaning services offer linen hire and laundering, swapping dirty sets for fresh ones each turnover so you're never waiting on a wash cycle. Ask your provider whether this is included.

Can a turnover really be done in two hours?

For a one or two-bedroom property in good condition with supplies pre-stocked, yes. Larger homes or properties needing a deeper reset will take longer, which is why starting laundry first and working to a checklist is essential.
