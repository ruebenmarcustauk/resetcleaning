How Much Does House Cleaning Cost on the Gold Coast? (2026)

If you're thinking about hiring a cleaner on the Gold Coast, the first question is almost always: what should this cost me? Prices vary based on the size of your home, the type of clean, and how often you book, so a single number rarely tells the whole story. This guide breaks down real 2026 Gold Coast cleaning prices, explains what drives them up or down, and helps you compare quotes with confidence.

Gold Coast house cleaning prices at a glance (2026)

Most Gold Coast cleaning companies charge somewhere between $36 and $67 per hour, with established providers sitting toward the higher end because they carry insurance, police-check their staff, and guarantee their work. Industry data puts the Gold Coast average at around $54 per hour in 2026. Many companies, though, prefer flat-rate pricing based on the number of bedrooms and bathrooms, which makes budgeting easier.

As a rough guide for a standard maintenance clean on the Gold Coast in 2026: a one-bedroom unit often starts around $130 to $160, a two-bedroom home runs roughly $160 to $240, and a larger three or four-bedroom home sits around $240 to $320. These are indicative ranges; your actual quote depends on the condition of the home and exactly what's included.

Pricing by type of clean

Regular (maintenance) cleaning

This is your recurring weekly or fortnightly clean to keep the home fresh. It's the cheapest per visit because the home never gets too far gone. Booking regularly is typically 10 to 20 percent cheaper per visit than a one-off, since a maintained home takes less time.

One-off and deep cleaning

A deep clean, sometimes called a spring clean, tackles built-up grime: inside appliances, detailed bathrooms, skirting boards, blinds and more. Because it involves far more scrubbing and detail, expect to pay more than a maintenance clean, often 1.5 to 2 times the regular rate for the same home.

End of lease / bond cleaning

Bond cleans are priced separately because of the detail and guarantee involved. On the Gold Coast in 2026, expect roughly $250 to $500 for a two-bedroom property and $350 to $700 for a three-bedroom home, with carpet steam cleaning often an add-on. A bond-back guarantee is worth paying for here.

What affects the price of your clean?

  • Home size: more rooms and more floor area mean more time.
  • Number of bathrooms: each bathroom adds 30 to 60 minutes, so this is a major cost driver.
  • Condition: a home cleaned regularly costs less than one that hasn't been touched in months.
  • Frequency: weekly and fortnightly cleans cost less per visit than one-offs.
  • Extras: oven, windows, fridge interior, walls, and carpet steam cleaning are often priced on top.
  • Access and location: hard-to-reach high-rises or outer suburbs can affect travel and time.

Hourly rate vs flat-rate pricing: which is better?

Hourly pricing can work out cheaper for small, well-maintained homes where the job is quick. Flat-rate (per-job) pricing gives you certainty and is usually better for larger homes or deep cleans, because you know the price upfront regardless of how long it takes. When comparing quotes, always check what's actually included so you're comparing like for like.

Pro tip

A suspiciously cheap quote often means an unvetted solo cleaner without insurance, or a price that balloons with 'extras' once they arrive. Always ask whether the quote is all-inclusive and whether the cleaner is insured.

How to get the best value (without sacrificing quality)

  • Book regularly: ongoing cleans are cheaper per visit and keep the home easier to maintain.
  • Bundle services: combining oven, windows and a general clean in one visit is often better value than separate bookings.
  • Declutter before the cleaner arrives so they spend time cleaning, not tidying.
  • Get at least two or three quotes and confirm exactly what each includes.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to provide cleaning products and equipment?

Most professional Gold Coast companies bring their own products and equipment. Always confirm when booking, especially if you prefer eco-friendly or fragrance-free products.

Should I tip my cleaner?

Tipping isn't expected in Australia, but it's always appreciated for a great job. A positive online review is just as valuable to a local business.

Is GST included in these prices?

Quotes from registered businesses include GST. Confirm this when comparing, as it affects the true cost.
